Cornhole: The Game of a Lifetime





Set Up

  1. Place Boards
    • Official distance is 27 ft
  2. Place Bags
    • Each player/team should have 4 bags each
    • begin with the bags all on one end
  3. Take Your Stance
    • I like to have my left foot forward
    • I also prefer to hold all unthrown bags in my non-throwing hand as I pitch the bags

Game Play

  1. Form Teams
    • Play singles (1v1) or doubles (2v2)
    • Teammates stand at opposite boards but on the same side of the boards
  2. Begin the Round
    • The last team/player to score goes first
    • Players alternate throws
    • Each player throws 4 bags
  3. Alternate Throws
    • Switch turns after each toss
    • Continue until all bags are thrown
  4. End the Round
    • Round ends after 8 total throws
    • Score is calculated at the end of the round

Technique

  1. Grip the Bag
    • Hold the bag with a relaxed grip
    • Keep your wrist firm
  2. Set Your Stance
    • Stand square or slightly angled to the board
      • I like to put my left foot forward
    • Keep your balance steady
  3. Do a Smooth Throw
    • Throw underhand with a fluid motion
    • For greatest consistency, spin the bag like a frisbee
      • Keep the bag on the same side of your body as you are throwing it with
      • Twist your arm as you go back, then untwist going forward
      • The bag should spin horazontally in the air and land flat
      • This approach minimized bouncing and lets you predict the slide of the bag more effectively
  4. Focus on Consistency
    • Aim for the same motion each throw
    • Control speed and landing

Scoring

  1. Count Bag Points Per Team
    • 3 points for a bag in the hole
    • 1 point for a bag on the board
  2. Use Cancellation Scoring (One team cancels out the other, or both cancel evenly)
    • Subtract the lower score from the higher
    • Only one team scores per round
  3. Track the Score
    • Add points after each round to the running total
  4. Win the Game
    • First team to reach 21 points wins
    • Game can go over 21 unless rules say exact
    • I think you should play exactly to 21, and if you go over, then you must go back down to 11 points

Celebrations for Winning

  1. Bet big and loud and make sure every one knows that you won (that you are the best)!
  2. Consider insulting your opponets' techinque, character, or mother
